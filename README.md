## 📌 Project Overview

This project implements a comprehensive **customer segmentation** and **churn risk analysis** framework using the **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)** methodology on an online retail transaction dataset. The primary objective is to transform raw transactional data into actionable business intelligence by identifying distinct customer groups, evaluating their revenue contribution, and detecting early warning signs of churn.

The analysis provides data-driven recommendations for marketing personalization, customer retention strategies, and resource allocation to maximize customer lifetime value (CLV). The entire workflow—from data cleaning to visualization—is executed in a Jupyter Notebook using Python's data science ecosystem.

## 📁 Dataset

- **Source**: [Online Retail II UCI Dataset](https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/Online+Retail+II)
- **Format**: Excel (.xlsx)
- **Records**: 525,461 transaction rows
- **Time Period**: December 2009 – December 2010
- **Key Columns**:
  - `Invoice`: Transaction identifier
  - `StockCode`: Product code
  - `Description`: Product description
  - `Quantity`: Units purchased (positive for sales, negative for returns/cancellations)
  - `InvoiceDate`: Timestamp of transaction
  - `Price`: Unit price
  - `Customer ID`: Unique customer identifier
  - `Country`: Customer country

## 🧠 Methodology

### 1. Data Preprocessing
- Removed duplicate records to ensure data integrity.
- Filtered out canceled orders (`Quantity < 0`).
- Excluded rows with missing `Customer ID` to maintain analytical consistency.
- Calculated `Revenue = Price × Quantity` for each transaction.
- Extracted `invoice_date_only` and `month` for temporal aggregation.

### 2. RFM Metric Calculation
RFM is a proven marketing technique that quantifies customer behavior along three dimensions:

| Metric | Definition | Computation |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Recency** | Time since the customer's last purchase | `(snapshot_date - max(invoice_date))` in days |
| **Frequency** | How often the customer makes purchases | `nunique(invoice_date)` per customer |
| **Monetary** | Total revenue generated by the customer | `sum(Revenue)` per customer |

- **Snapshot Date** = `max(invoice_date) + 1 day` to compute recency.

### 3. RFM Scoring
Each metric was discretized into quartile-based scores (1–4 or 1–3) to enable consistent segmentation:
- **Recency Score**: Lower recency → higher score (4 = most recent).
- **Frequency Score**: Higher frequency → higher score.
- **Monetary Score**: Higher revenue → higher score.

### 4. Customer Segmentation
Based on RFM scores, customers were classified into six actionable segments:

| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Champions** | High Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (top performers) |
| **Loyal Customers** | Good Recency and Frequency; consistent buyers |
| **New Customers** | Recent purchasers with low Frequency |
| **Need Attention** | Low Recency but high Monetary; at risk of churn |
| **Need Engagement** | Moderate Recency and Monetary; require activation |
| **Lost Customers** | Low Recency and Frequency; likely churned |

### 5. Churn Risk Classification
A secondary risk-tiering model was developed to identify customers at varying degrees of churn probability:

| Risk Level | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **Already Churned** | Recency > 180 days |
| **High Risk (At Risk)** | Recency > 90 days, high F & M scores |
| **Medium Risk (Need Attention)** | Recency > 60 days, medium F & M |
| **Low Risk (Active)** | Recency < 60 days, high Frequency |
| **Low Activity** | Fallback category for infrequent buyers |

## 📈 Key Insights & Business Impact

### Revenue Concentration
- **Top 20 customers** account for **~22%** of total revenue.
- **Champions** (only 10.7% of total customers) generate **50.8%** of total revenue—highlighting the critical importance of retention strategies for this segment.

### Churn Exposure
- **19.1%** of customers are already churned (no purchase in >6 months).
- **High Risk** customers (1.3% of base) represent a substantial revenue loss opportunity if not re‑engaged, with an average revenue of $3,091 per customer.

### Segment Characteristics
| Segment | % of Customers | % of Revenue | Avg. Revenue | Avg. Frequency | Avg. Recency (days) |
|---------|---------------|--------------|--------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Champions | 10.7% | 50.8% | $9,666 | 13.5 | 7.5 |
| Loyal | 9.7% | 14.1% | $2,946 | 7.6 | 24.4 |
| Need Attention | 4.0% | 7.2% | $3,647 | 7.2 | 99.4 |
| Need Engagement | 32.2% | 18.5% | $1,174 | 2.3 | 73.3 |
| New Customers | 11.5% | 4.8% | $858 | 2.4 | 10.4 |
| Lost Customers | 31.9% | 4.7% | $300 | 1.4 | 185.8 |

## 💡 Recommendations

1. **Retain Champions** – Offer exclusive loyalty programs, early access to new products, and personalized communications to maintain high engagement.
2. **Re‑engage High Risk & Need Attention** – Deploy win‑back campaigns (e.g., personalized discounts or targeted email reminders) to prevent revenue leakage.
3. **Nurture New & Need Engagement** – Implement onboarding sequences and educational content to increase purchase frequency and move customers up the loyalty ladder.
4. **Optimize Marketing Spend** – Allocate budget proportionally to segment value; avoid over‑investing in Lost Customers.
5. **Monitor Churn Signals** – Set up automated alerts for Recency exceeding 60 days to trigger timely interventions.
